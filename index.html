About Me

Who Am I?

I'm a student studying in Chiang Mai Thailand with an interest in maths, physics, computer science, music, and I also compete regionally in swimming competitions in Thailand.

Computer science

I started learning computer science by building a redstone computer in Minecraft, which introduced me to CPU architecture, ALUs, registers, RAM, and ASICs. I then learned Python, creating small math programs for school, such as solving quadratics and binomial expansions. My early programs focused on mathematical concepts like Taylor series, CORDIC, and finding primes. I also made apps like a Todo app and a simple renderer to apply linear algebra and trigonometry. I began learning C++ for better performance, studying OpenGL and raytracing through TheCherno's YouTube channel. I expanded my raytracer to Vulkan and made it cross-platform with Metal, and developed a pathtracer inspired by Sebastian Lague's videos. I’ve also worked on projects including a compiler, interpreter, text editor, and physics engine. Currently, I’m working on a neural network from scratch in C++, cross-platform Metal support for my renderer, heat and fluid simulations, and I plan to learn web development and networking soon.

Experiences

CMU

Over one of my summer break, I did a month long internship/research at Chiang Mai University (CMU) in the Environmental Science Research Center (ESRC) focusing on air pollution in Chiang Mai. I did research on PM toxicity, PM trajectories, atmospheric simulations, and wrote a paper on the work I did on PM toxicity.

Summer festival

I hosted a rock concert featuring 4 different bands to mark the end of the school year. I learned to organize events with many different people: the school event manager, school principle, the bands, food vendors, security, and many more.

Claron McFadden

I got to work with Grammy nomincated vocalist Claron McFadden where I accompanied her on Piano. We did a couple pieces together as well as a couple improvised pieces together.

Weather ballons

I worked on launching weather ballons in Chiang Mai for the purpose of measuring particulate matter in the air and detecting for an inversion layer in the atmosphere. This inversion layer plays a large role in the entrapment of particulate matter in Chiang Mai due to the surrounding mountains. I worked with the meterological center and the National Astronomical Research Institute of Thailand (NARIT).
